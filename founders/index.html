Founders

Four founding members, four forces.

ADPS is not a single-author hero narrative. It is a small engineering community carrying Chinese practitioners' field experience into the global conversation about agent architecture. The four founding members each anchor one of the forces this work needs: architecture language, software engineering, open-source ecosystem, enterprise deployment.

Jia Huang (黄佳)

FRAMEWORK · WRITING · GLOBAL NARRATIVE

AI researcher at A*STAR Singapore. Author of Manning's Designing AI Agents, the Chinese Agent 设计模式, and the Geekbang course Claude Code 工程化实战.

Role in ADPS: framework proposer, pattern-language curator, global narrative interface.

Key contributions:

  • Proposed and published the two-axis framework: cognitive function × execution topology.
  • Organized 28 agent design patterns into a discussable, reviewable, transferable architecture language.
  • Carried Chinese engineering practice to a global audience through the Chinese book, Geekbang course, enterprise training, Manning MEAP, the arXiv paper, and a personal site.

Bingsheng Ru (茹炳晟)

SOFTWARE ENGINEERING · R&D PRODUCTIVITY · REAL PROJECTS

Tech Lead at Tencent. Special researcher at Tencent Research Institute. Member of the Tencent Group Technical Committee and the Fudan University CodeWisdom team. A leading practitioner and communicator in software engineering, R&D productivity, and AI-augmented development in China.

Also serves as: Chair of the CCF TF R&D Productivity SIG; Mentor in Tencent Cloud's Architect Technical Alliance; Deputy Lead of the Cloud Software Engineering Working Group in CCSA TC608. Regular technical conference producer, co-chair, keynote speaker, and book author/translator.

Role in ADPS: software engineering methodology anchor; perspective from R&D productivity and complex-system evaluation.

Key contributions:

  • Grounds ADPS in real software-development settings instead of toy problems and demos.
  • Brings R&D productivity, software complexity, organizational collaboration, and engineering evaluation perspectives that constrain the pattern catalog with realism.
  • Connects ADPS to CodeWisdom, Tencent, and the broader Chinese software-engineering community — extending the work beyond the AI subculture.

Willem (Ning) Jiang (姜宁)

OPEN SOURCE · HARNESS · WORKFLOW

Long-time Apache Software Foundation contributor. Key member of the Apache Camel community. Active open-source practitioner. Listed as a DeerFlow developer; involved in DeerFlow 2.0 public activities.

Role in ADPS: open-source harness practitioner; the engineering bridge between multi-agent design and runnable workflow infrastructure.

Key contributions:

  • Connects the ADPS pattern language to runnable, deployable, community-collaborated harness engineering.
  • Brings cross-generational engineering experience — from Apache Camel to CAMEL-AI / DeerFlow — across routing, orchestration, integration, workflow, and community governance.
  • Ensures agent design patterns don't stay confined to books and talks but enter open-source projects, developer communities, and real toolchains.

Bo Liang (梁博)

ENTERPRISE SAAS · FINTECH · PRODUCTION FEEDBACK

Vice President, co-founder, and founding shareholder of Shanghai Dongfang Yiteng Technology Co., Ltd., responsible for technology and product. The Dongfang Yiteng core team comes from the banking internet-channel domain and focuses on SaaS services in the banking and broader-finance ecosystem. Its Xinxiangtong SaaS matrix has joint-operation arrangements with 20+ banks, serves 50,000+ enterprise customers, and is undergoing a full transformation from a SaaS product matrix to an agents-based system.

Role in ADPS: enterprise deployment feedback; FinTech SaaS-to-agent transformation; source of real business constraints.

Key contributions:

  • Validates the ADPS pattern language inside complex business flows: banking and broader-finance, HR SaaS, payroll, attendance, tax, disbursement.
  • Supplies the SaaS-to-agents transformation scenario — not "add a chat entry point," but rebuilding enterprise service flows into systems that are budgetable, observable, authorizable, and reviewable.
  • Continuously brings real enterprise problems, architectural feedback, and product-boundary constraints back into the framework.

The four forces, combined

The four founders together represent four forces:

  1. Architecture language — sediment messy experience into a two-axis framework, a pattern matrix, and design philosophy.
  2. Software engineering — place agents back into R&D productivity, complex systems, and real project evaluation.
  3. Open-source ecosystem — connect the pattern language to runnable harnesses, community collaboration, and engineering implementations.
  4. Enterprise deployment — feed back real constraints from banking, broader-finance, SaaS, and organizational transformation into the framework.
